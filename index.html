    • San Jose, Costa Rica (capital city)

    San Jose is the capital of Costa Rica and is in the cental valley. It is a vibrant city full of interesting people and things to do. While there, we went on a "Bites & Sites" tour, as well as made our own chocolate bars. The guide introduced us to many places and a variety of delicous food.

    Chocoate Tour in San Jose

    San Pedro (where I lived and worked)

    San Pedro is next to San Jose, easily within walking distance to many of the fun places in the capital.
    It is where the language institute is located, where I taught ESL.

    Guanacaste

    Guanacaste is known for its beautiful beaches along the Paciifc coast, with an abundance of hotels, cabins and restaurants.
    A great place to try 'la typical comida' - a typical CR lunch - which is a delicious combination of flavors and texture.
    It's name, 'Casado', actually means 'married' because the flavors complement each other so wonderfully.

    Manual Antonio National Park

    Known as the 'gem' of the national parks in CR, it does not disappoint! Go here
    to see all kinds of delightful flora and fauna. Hire a guide to take you through the park -
    they train for years so they can inform you and help you find (zoom in with their powerful scopes),
    the capuchin monkeys, both 2- and 3-toed sloths, and so much more!

    Arenal Volcano

    Until 2010, Arenal was the most active volcano. It is huge, it is symmetrical, and it is worth seeing.
    Nearby, you can enjoy hot springs, hiking, fishing, and floating along beautiful rivers.

    La Fortuna

    This incredible and powerful waterfall is 70 meters (almost 300 feet) high and races down to the large, deep pool below.
